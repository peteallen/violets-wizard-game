# Chapter 3 — First Classes

The Chapter 3 detail pass. This is the first chapter where Violet stops being a
guest in a personalized story and starts using learned verbs to change the
world. It introduces the learning layer without ever looking or sounding like
an educational app.

## Chapter promise

- **Story promise:** learn real magic, then use it to help someone.
- **Mechanical promise:** the spellbook arrives; spells become permanent world
  verbs.
- **Learning promise:** letters and syllables produce visible magic. No quiz,
  score, grade, or correction voice.
- **Emotional promise:** class is exciting, the corridors are cozy, Trevor is
  found, and Violet is never made to feel stuck.

Target length: 20–30 minutes, with safe scene-boundary saves after each class
spell and each corridor. The chapter can comfortably span two sessions.

## Frustration budget

Violet is impatient and may leave as soon as a game makes her feel unable to do
the obvious next thing. Chapter 3 therefore uses a faster, local hint ladder
than ordinary exploration:

1. The intended action is visually legible immediately.
2. After **3 seconds** without progress, the next useful object breathes or
   shimmers.
3. After **one wrong attempt**, the Charms Professor responds in fiction and
   demonstrates the relevant sound or action again. No failure label appears.
4. After **two wrong attempts or 6 seconds**, all irrelevant choices dim and the
   answer glows.
5. After **three wrong attempts or 9 seconds**, the next tap anywhere relevant
   completes the step.

Tap-spamming is accepted as a strategy. It produces funny reactions but always
converges. No animation locks input for more than one second, dialogue can be
advanced, and neither assembly can reset.

## Educational minigame rules

The class contains two different kinds of play so the learning layer does not
feel like a repeated exercise.

### Lumos: letter-to-sound matching disguised as charging a wand

The parchment already shows five large recessed rune slots spelling **LUMOS**.
This is matching, not spelling from memory.

- The professor says the whole incantation once: “Lumos.”
- Each slot wakes from left to right. Two or three rune tiles float nearby; the
  correct tile has the same silhouette as the slot.
- Tapping a tile speaks its **letter sound**, not a school-style prompt. The
  correct tile lands in the slot, plays the next note of a rising melody, and
  brightens Violet's wand.
- A wrong tile does something entertaining—a tiny puff, hiccup, or squeak—then
  remains available. The professor never says “wrong,” “try again,” or names the
  educational skill.
- The first position offers only **L** so the interaction teaches itself. The
  middle positions offer one distractor. The final **S** may offer two only if
  the hidden Gentle-dial signal says she has been moving confidently.
- On completion the five notes resolve, Violet says “Lumos!”, and the classroom
  briefly dims so her wand can bloom warm white.

The reward is continuous: every correct letter makes the wand visibly more
magical. The game never waits until the end to tell her she succeeded.

### Wingardium Leviosa: syllable rhythm disguised as performing the scene

This is not a second spelling test. The enormous incantation arrives as six
illustrated chant tiles:

**WIN · GAR · DIUM · LEVI · O · SA**

- The professor chants the phrase once while the tiles pulse in sequence.
- The tiles then drift around the feather. Violet taps the currently glowing
  tile; it speaks its syllable and joins the ribbon.
- Tapping a different tile makes it sing its own syllable and bob comically, but
  the expected tile glows immediately. There is no loss of progress.
- Each landed syllable adds lift: the feather twitches, rolls, rises a little,
  and finally sails overhead on **SA**.
- The professor's familiar correction is about performance—“Swish and flick!”—
  never literacy.
- Completion is guaranteed and extravagant: harp gliss, gold ribbons, maximum
  sparkle, delighted classmates, and Violet's feather rising cleanly on the
  first completed chant.

The hidden practice is hearing a long word as manageable spoken parts. Violet
experiences it as saying the famous spell correctly.

## Scene flow

### C3-S1 — Owl post and the first spellbook

**Room:** her common-room corner, autumn morning.

An owl delivers a short letter from home and a wrapped spellbook bearing her
name and house colors. The letter's recap is voiced and optional. Opening the
book reveals empty illustrated card frames; the Charms classroom location
appears on the castle map with a gold star.

**Player-visible flow:** tap owl → open parcel → spellbook fans once → tap the
starred classroom.

**Purpose:** make the new UI feel like a present, not a menu tutorial.

### C3-S2 — Charms: Lumos

**Room:** bright Charms classroom. Feather piles, stacked books, warm autumn
light. The room may dim only for the Lumos payoff.

1. The Charms Professor greets Violet by name and produces a dark little lantern
   that has “forgotten how to shine.”
2. Violet taps the lantern; her empty spellbook opens to a new page.
3. Run the Lumos assembly above.
4. The lantern answers her completed cast and glows. The **Lumos** card settles
   permanently into the spellbook.
5. One free cast follows: the professor darkens the room, the lantern target
   shimmers, and Violet uses the normal spellbook casting flow to light it.

**Core voice/caption beats:**

- Professor: “Every witch needs a light.” / **A light!**
- Professor: “Letters into magic. Listen…” / **Lumos**
- Violet: “Lumos!” / **Lumos!**
- Professor: “Splendid! You woke the light.” / **It shines!**

The free cast proves that assembly was the one-time learning ceremony and icon
casting is how mastered magic works afterward.

### C3-S3 — Charms: Wingardium Leviosa

1. The professor sets a feather in front of Violet and performs the familiar
   swish-and-flick demonstration.
2. Run the syllable-rhythm sequence above.
3. The feather's motion escalates with every syllable and completes SP-07 on the
   final tile.
4. **Wingardium Leviosa** enters the spellbook as a feather-icon card labeled
   simply **Leviosa** at normal gameplay size; the full incantation remains on
   its detail page and in optional wand practice.

**Core voice/caption beats:**

- Professor: “Long spells come in little pieces.” / **Listen close**
- Professor: “Swish and flick!” / **Swish! Flick!**
- Violet: “Wingardium Leviosa!” / **Leviosa!**
- Professor: “Up she goes!” / **You did it!**

The celebration is in-world excitement, not praise for decoding syllables.

**Save boundary:** class complete; both spells persist.

### C3-S4 — Trevor is missing

Outside class, a Neville-shaped child is tearful but not panicked. Trevor has
escaped into the night corridors. Violet receives one clear voiced objective:
“Please help me find Trevor.” The quest-star caption is **Find Trevor**.

The professor reminds her that some creatures hide in dark places. The map
opens with the first corridor starred.

### C3-S5 — Corridor one: prove Lumos

**Room:** velvet-blue corridor, silhouettes always readable. One dark alcove is
visible near the far end.

- On entry, Violet's pet looks at the dark alcove.
- Walking near it produces one soft croak and a Lumos-shaped glint at its edge.
- Open spellbook → choose Lumos → tap alcove. The light reveals wet little
  footprints, not Trevor.
- The footprints point toward the next doorway; the map star advances.

There is no decoy spell target. This room is a confidence check, not a puzzle.

### C3-S6 — Corridor two: look closely

Two alcoves sit along the corridor. Lumos may be cast on either, and both casts
produce something worthwhile:

- first alcove: a hidden chocolate frog card and a louder croak from farther on;
- second alcove: a dropped toad-sized ribbon or scarf scrap that confirms the
  trail.

The order does not matter. The exit glows after either clue; finding both is
optional. The room quietly teaches that exploration can pay off even when an
object is not the quest answer.

### C3-S7 — Corridor three: find Trevor

Three cozy dark shapes are visible: armor, a curtain, and an alcove. Croaks pan
toward the alcove when audio is available; a tiny pair of reflected eyes blinks
there for visual parity.

Lumos on the alcove reveals Trevor. Any other inspection gets a one-line comic
reaction and makes the correct alcove shimmer immediately. Violet taps Trevor;
he hops into her hands and says one indignant croak.

Violet: “Found you!” / **Found Trevor!**

**Save boundary:** Trevor found.

### C3-S8 — Return and first side quest

Returning Trevor completes the main quest. His owner hugs him, Violet earns
house points, and a small toad token appears in her dormitory collection.

On the walk back, a friendly ghost calls from a wall portrait. His favorite book
has torn pages and he asks Violet to remember him if she ever learns a mending
spell. This is the first optional side quest:

- it enters the quest list as **Fix the book**;
- it is explicitly marked with a sleeping silver star, not the active gold main
  quest star;
- no current spell applies, so the book does not shimmer as a cast target;
- Reparo in Chapter 5 wakes the quest automatically.

The ghost gives one frog card for listening, so accepting an unresolved quest
still feels rewarding now.

### C3-S9 — Chapter close

Back in the common room, the spellbook opens to show Lumos and Leviosa together.
The chapter card reads **Violet's First Spells**. Autumn arrives visibly in the
map and common-room window. Owl post teases flying lessons.

## State and interaction contract

| State | Required player action | Completion flag | Hint convergence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spellbook parcel | Tap parcel/book | `ch3.spellbookOpened` | parcel pulses; pet taps it |
| Lumos assembly | Land five rune tiles | `ch3.lumosLearned` | 3/6/9-second class ladder |
| Lumos free cast | Cast on lantern | `ch3.lumosProved` | lantern + spell card shimmer |
| Leviosa chant | Land six syllable tiles | `ch3.leviosaLearned` | 3/6/9-second class ladder |
| Accept Trevor quest | Advance voiced request | `ch3.toadQuestAccepted` | quest star repeats line |
| Corridor one | Lumos the alcove | `ch3.trailFound` | pet look → glint → auto-highlight |
| Corridor two | Find either clue | `ch3.corridorClueFound` | both alcoves become valid |
| Corridor three | Reveal and tap Trevor | `ch3.toadFound` | croak/eyes → shimmer → completion |
| Return Trevor | Talk to owner | `ch3.toadReturned` | map and quest star point back |
| Ghost request | Hear or skip request | `side.ghostBookAccepted` | optional; never blocks chapter |

Exact flag names are proposed documentation, not a demand to change existing
contracts if the implementation already has canonical equivalents.

## Parent-dial behavior

- **Off:** Lumos completes with five large tiles arriving in order on taps;
  Leviosa remains the syllable performance because it is spectacle, not a test.
- **Gentle (default):** matching layout described above; at most one distractor
  until the final letter.
- **Stretchy:** later Lumos positions may offer two distractors and one recessed
  slot may briefly hide its printed letter while retaining its silhouette.

The dial changes assistance, never dialogue, rewards, story state, or the size
of the celebration.

## Playtest observations

Do not ask whether the minigame was educational or fun. Watch:

- Does she tap the correct Lumos tile by matching, or spam all tiles?
- Does she imitate the letter sounds or the Leviosa chant aloud?
- Does a wrong-tile joke make her deliberately try another wrong tile? That is
  success, not misuse.
- Does she reopen wand practice voluntarily?
- Does she use Lumos in corridor one without prompting?
- At any point does she lean back, hand the device over, or say she cannot do it?
  If yes, shorten the hint timing or remove choices before adding explanation.

The chapter passes when she experiences herself as learning magic. Literacy and
phonological practice are the machinery under the floor.
