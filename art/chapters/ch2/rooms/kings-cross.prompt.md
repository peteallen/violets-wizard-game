# Chapter 2 King's Cross concourse

Model: `google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image`

Provider: `google-vertex/global`

Endpoint: OpenRouter `POST /images`

Requested delivery: one 16:9 room painting at 2048×1152. The live Lite endpoint
currently produces a native 1K source, which is center-cropped and resized once
to the exact delivery dimensions before WebP optimization.

## Prompt

Children's storybook illustration, painterly gouache and watercolor, soft edges and gentle texture, warm candlelit golds against deep night blues, cozy magical atmosphere, richly detailed but uncluttered, straight-on side view like a theater stage set, floor plane along the bottom quarter of the frame, no people, no creatures, no text or lettering.

Use case: illustration-story. Asset type: full-screen gameplay room painting for the ordinary side of a magical railway barrier.

Paint an original grand British railway concourse in late-summer morning light, seen broadside at child height. Tall soot-softened brick arches and iron-and-glass roof trusses recede gently without becoming a realistic photograph. Warm globe lamps glow against cool blue-gray station shadows and faint pearly steam. On the right-center, make one substantial section of honey-red brick wall between two arches visually clear and inviting: it is the magical barrier Violet will run through. The masonry must be continuous and solid, with no doorway, crack, portal glow, or preview of the platform beyond it. Place a few empty brass luggage trolleys and neatly stacked old trunks far enough to the sides that they frame rather than block the barrier.

Keep the left side open for Violet's entrance and leave a broad calm stone floor across the bottom quarter for walking and character overlays. Preserve open space immediately before the right-center barrier for the approach and running animation. Use an authored warm upper-left key light, deep ink-blue shadows beneath the arches, irregular gouache brush texture, paper tooth, soft dark-brown painted edges, and small amber reflections on the floor. The station should feel busy in its architecture but mysteriously paused for Violet's private moment.

No passengers, porters, silhouettes, faces, animals, birds, statues of creatures, advertisements, flags, logos, railway company marks, clocks with legible numerals, platform numbers, signs, labels, letters, watermark, signature, film still, copied movie composition, photorealism, 3D rendering, flat vector art, pure black, fisheye lens, or tilted camera. Do not put magical light on the brick barrier; the runtime effect supplies the reveal.
