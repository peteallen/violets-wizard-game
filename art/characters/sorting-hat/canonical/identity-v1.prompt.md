stool, mannequin head, school crest, buckle, feather, patch lettering, or a
second hat.

side. The object must read cleanly at small gameplay size, with a strong
asymmetrical silhouette, a clearly legible face, and no thin floating scraps.
Match reference 1's richly painted opaque gouache-and-watercolor picture-book
finish: visible paper and felt tooth, soft dark-brown contour accents dissolving
into paint, organic repair stitches, warm upper-left gold light, and cool
violet-blue shadows. Keep it safe and friendly for a six-year-old, never
sinister, monstrous, photoreal, or actor-like.

Place the Hat on reference 2's perfectly flat uniform solid cyan field. The
background has no gradient, texture, floor plane, shadow, reflection, scenery,
decoration, magical glow, sparks, or lighting variation. Never use cyan in the

fragments, text, letters, numbers, logo, watermark, signature, border, panel,
anime, vector art, 3D rendering, plastic texture, pure black voids, or hard ink
